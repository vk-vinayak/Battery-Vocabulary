Battery Passport Vocabulary

Battery Chemistry

It defines chemistry of cells inside the battery pack. Example: NMC, LFP etc

Name of the cathode, anode, electrolyte materials

String:Component materials used : Naming the materials (as a composition of substances) in cathode, anode, electrolyte according to public standards, including specification of the corresponding component (i.e., cathode, anode, or electrolyte). We suggest a reporting threshold of 0.1 % weight by weight.

Related identifiers of the cathode, anode, electrolyte materials

ID: Component materials used : CAS numbers of the materials (as a composition of substances) in cathode, anode, electrolyte.

in grams

in ohm

Manual for disassembly and dismantling of the battery pack

Dismantling information, including at least: - Exploded diagrams of the battery system/pack showing the location of battery cells - Disassembly sequences - Type and number of fastening techniques to be unlocked - Tools required for disassembly - Warnings if risk of damaging parts exists - Amount of cells used and layout

Battery Status

Mention at which lifecycle stage the battery currently is.E.g. First use/second use/recycling etc.

Capacity Fade

%: Remaining capacity/Rated capacity

State of Charge (SOC)

%: %: available capacity/rated capacity e.g. If SOC is 100% means the battery is fully charged and 0% means the battery is completely drained.

Power capability fade

Loss of battery power compared to begining of life

Loss of round trip energy efficiency compared to its begining of life
